Reflections - Design and Analysis of Algorithms

Introduction

The Design and Analysis of Algorithms (DAA) course focuses on understanding and creating efficient solutions for computational problems. It combines data structures and algorithm design techniques to solve real-world challenges.

Course Learning Reflections

Kinds of Problems

We often encounter problems like iteration (repeating a task), recursion (breaking a task into smaller tasks), and backtracking (trying different options). For example, solving a Sudoku puzzle involves backtracking.

Space and Time Efficiency

Space and time efficiency help determine how fast and memory-efficient an algorithm is. For example, sorting a list of marks can be done using bubble sort for small datasets or quicksort for larger datasets.

Design Techniques

Reflection Questions

How do you determine the most efficient approach when solving a complex problem? By analyzing the input size, constraints, and selecting an algorithm with the lowest time complexity.

Reflect on a situation where you balanced conflicting constraints in a design: For example, while working on a group project, we divided tasks based on individual strengths, balancing speed and quality.

How do you identify patterns in datasets or problems? By visualizing relationships (e.g., graphs or trees) and identifying repeating structures.

How do you decide when to prioritize simplicity over optimization? For smaller datasets, simplicity is better, such as using bubble sort for sorting 10 numbers instead of quicksort.
